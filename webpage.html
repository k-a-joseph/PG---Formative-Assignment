What is sustainable fashion?

Sustainable fashion is an umbrella term that refers to ethical and environmently-friendly fashion industry. It encompasses responsible sourcing, manufacturing, distributing and consumption practices.

How can you participate in sustainable fashion as an individual?

  1. Buy second-hand.
    Instead of purchasing new clothes, consider buying from second-hand and charity shops. This a good way to get something new while having minimal negative on the planet. This is also a way to give back to the community as most thrift stores are associated with a charity, who recieve a percentage of their profit. Additionally, you can get high-quality clothing in good condition for a fraction of what you would pay if you were buying it brand new.
  2. Trade or borrow clothes from friends/family.
    Raid the closets of your friends and family to see if they have any pieces of clothing they are willing to trade, borrow or give to you, or vice versa. It's a win-win for everyone.
  3. Buy less clothes
    Think twice buying something new. Consider if you actually need this piece. Can you use something you already own instead? Can you repair what you already have? Can you borrow it someone instead? Will you actually use this piece? How much use would you get out of this piece? These are all important questions to ask yourself before you buy something new.
    Advice: Wait at least two weeks before buying something new. If you still want/need it after two weeks, then buy it.
  4. Buy from sustainable brands
    If you are going to buy something brand new, consider buying it from brands that have sustainable business practices, from sourcing materials to when you purchase an item in their stores.
  5. Buy quality clothes
    Quality over quantity; invest in quality pieces that would last you longer. Purchase a handfull of well made things instead of buy a lot of cheaper alternatives.

How can companies in the fashion industry be more sustainable?

  1. Use of environmentally-friendly materials
    Companies can use natural/organic materials (linen, wool, leather and cotton) instead of synthetic materials (polyester, acrlic and nylon) as the natural materials are biodegradable and have minimal negative impact on the environment.
  2. Ethical treatment of workers
    The fashion industry, especially the fast fashion industry, violate the basic human rights of millions of people across the world. Some of these violations include horrible working conditions, child labour, modern-day slavery and unfair wages.
  3. Recycle synthetic fabrics
    Exsisting synthetic material can be recycled to create new graments. This reduces the amount of synthetic fabric that are incinerated or end up in landfills. This also eliminates the need to manufacture new synthetic fabric to construct the garment, which reduces the environmental impact. As the manufacturing of synthetic fabrics is a major contributor to global pollution, water consumption and contamination and energy consumption.
  4. Reduce the usage of toxic chemicals
    The fashion industry use many toxic chemicals in the growing of raw materials, the dyeing of the fabric, etc. The use of these harmful chemicals which have a detrimental effects on the envrionment.
